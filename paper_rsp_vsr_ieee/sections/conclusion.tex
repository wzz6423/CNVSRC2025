\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This study evaluated sparse-feedback continual personalization for Mandarin VSR under a strict predict-then-update protocol. A 75k-parameter residual adapter with one correction every ten utterances reduced Chinese-LiPS CER by 2.42 absolute points relative to the frozen source model and remained stable across three seeds. The result is practically useful: most of the attainable gain came from a simple adapter and a clear feedback contract.

The experiments do not support the stronger structural story. The original dynamic expert bank improved over one adapter by only 0.055 CER points on average and assigned 99.87\% of test-revisit samples to one expert. A validation-only calibration passed its signature proxy gate but then created eight experts, reused the A1-dominant expert on only 42.3\% of A2, and performed significantly worse than one adapter. Reliability and rollback ablations likewise yielded effects too small for a strong causal safety claim. We therefore reject those claims rather than interpreting complexity as evidence.

Before submission, the study still requires matched standard TTA and VSR-personalization baselines, a second real shift type beyond speakers, complete parameter/latency/memory accounting, and final figures. The resolved validation gate removes expert growth from the proposed deployment path; the paper instead presents the simpler sparse-feedback adapter system with its measured limitations and the expert result as a controlled negative finding.
