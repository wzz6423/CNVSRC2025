\section{Results and Analysis}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Online Adaptation and Sparse Feedback Both Improve CER}

Table~\ref{tab:main} summarizes the primary operating points on the complete test stream. No-feedback online adaptation reaches $67.713\pm0.178$\% CER across three seeds, 1.589 points below static. Adding feedback every ten samples lowers the matched three-seed mean by a further 0.832 points and yields a 2.421-point mean gain over static. The feedback-every-ten population standard deviation is 0.105 points, and all three matched seed comparisons favor feedback. More frequent corrections also matter: the expert-bank mean improves by 0.490 points from feedback every 20 to every 10 samples.

\subsection{Two Intuitive Update Alternatives Fail on Validation}

Full-sequence feedback replay reaches 69.637\% CER on the 641-utterance validation revisit, improving the 72.329\% static result by 2.692 points (paired 95\% CI: $-3.315$ to $-2.070$). Preserving greedily matched spans appears more selective, but reaches 70.174\% and is 0.538 points worse than replay (95\% CI: $+0.074$ to $+1.077$). The greedy correct-span variant therefore fails its validation gate and is not evaluated on test.

Adapter-only entropy minimization is substantially less stable. Selecting all CTC frames produces 98.581\% CER, while selecting only nonblank greedy frames produces 96.742\%. Relative to static, the two variants degrade CER by 26.252 and 24.413 points, respectively, and both paired intervals lie wholly in the harmful direction. We stop this baseline after validation rather than tune it against test outcomes. These failures do not imply that all TTA methods fail for VSR; they show that direct entropy minimization of this frozen-backbone adapter is not a competitive control.

\subsection{Error Localization Is Informative but Underperforms Replay}

Table~\ref{tab:validation_local} resolves the final validation-only update gate. Error localization reaches 72.004\% CER. It is 2.508 points better than randomized support (95\% CI: $-3.087$ to $-1.955$), showing that where the sparse correction gradient acts contains useful information. However, it is 2.367 points worse than full-sequence replay (95\% CI: $+1.757$ to $+3.048$). Its revisit difference-in-differences is also 3.244 points worse than replay (95\% CI: $+1.527$ to $+5.044$). Relative to static, the 0.325-point CER improvement is not significant (95\% CI: $-0.844$ to $+0.227$). The pre-specified replay gate therefore fails and no localized test run, parameter adjustment, or additional seed is conducted.

All 64 scheduled corrections receive a target-conditioned occupancy. Thus local objective descent is not sufficient evidence of future transcript improvement, and whole-sequence replay remains the selected update rule.

\subsection{Dynamic Experts Do Not Beat One Adapter Materially}

At feedback every ten samples, the expert advantage over one adapter is 0.087, 0.025, and 0.053 CER points for seeds 7, 42, and 123, respectively, for a mean of only 0.055 points. This is below the 0.3-point materiality threshold used for the structural decision. Because these prototype runs allowed the known feedback-location flag to confirm growth before the corresponding prediction, we do not treat the expert rows as strict causal comparisons; the negative routing and accuracy outcomes are diagnostic despite that favorable information.

The revisit stream leads to the same conclusion (Table~\ref{tab:revisit}). Both adaptive systems improve over static. The expert bank is only 0.085 points better overall than one adapter and reduces static-corrected forgetting by 0.156 points (lower is better). Both effects remain below the 0.3-point structural threshold. More importantly, 741 of 742 clips route to expert~0. Thus the observed difference cannot support domain-specialized reuse.

The repair nevertheless fails both downstream gates. \subsection{Noise Robustness Is Observed but Not Attributed}

With feedback every ten samples, one controlled seed-42 run with 10\% feedback-character replacement changes CER from 66.728\% to 66.770\%, a degradation of 0.041 points. At feedback every 20, the noisy run is 0.023 points better than clean, but this single-seed difference is smaller than the clean three-seed spread and is not interpreted as an improvement. Under that noisy setting, disabling rollback and reliability worsens CER by 0.039 and 0.076 points relative to the full system. These effects are too small to establish robustness or attribute it to either mechanism; repeated corruptions and equivalence intervals remain necessary.

\subsection{Development-Only Partial Update-Source Comparison on Target-Dev2}

Target-dev2 is an isolated development stream drawn from the official Chinese-LiPS train pool (speakers 015/098/133; 804 clips; A--B--C--A lengths 154/253/243/154). All numbers below are development-only exploratory evidence; they are not test or holdout results, and target-holdout2 remains unread.

On the feedback-every-ten schedule, combined full-sequence replay reaches $60.1061\%$ CER ($13{,}373/22{,}249$ character errors) with accepted/rolled-back/skipped updates $107/2/695$. Feedback-only reaches $60.3218\%$ CER ($13{,}421/22{,}249$) with $78/2/724$ updates; all $724$ non-feedback samples are update-forbidden under that schedule. The paired difference feedback-only $-$ combined is $+0.2157$ CER percentage points, with a paired bootstrap 95\% CI of $[-0.0644,+0.4927]$ points that includes zero. Returning-A (A2) CERs are $52.0945\%$ for feedback-only and $50.8346\%$ for combined. On the revisit difference-of-differences criterion, feedback-only $-$ combined is $+1.0013$ points with 95\% CI $[-0.3415,+2.2740]$ points, again including zero. Exact machine-readable values are retained in the archived analysis artifact.

These runs constitute a partial update-source comparison only: the same stream lacks a pseudo-only arm, so the design cannot support a complete pseudo/feedback/interaction split, nor a claim that reliability-gated pseudo updates yield a significant gain over feedback-only.

On the same target-dev2 stream, a full-replay CTC-error hybrid was evaluated against the full-replay baseline and a matched random-support hybrid control (development-only; strictly accepted protocol checks include 804 UID/order, 80 feedback positions, localization strategy/auxiliary objectives, 33 history, final checkpoint processed-samples count of 804, manifest/sidecar/base hashes, three checkpoints, attempt~1, and zero errors). Full-replay baseline CER is $60.1061\%$ ($13{,}373/22{,}249$). CTC-error hybrid reaches $60.4027\%$ ($13{,}439/22{,}249$) with accepted/skipped updates $109/695$. Random-support hybrid reaches $64.4433\%$ ($14{,}338/22{,}249$) with accepted/rolled-back/skipped $49/53/702$. The paired difference hybrid $-$ full is $+0.2966$ CER percentage points (hybrid worse on the point estimate), with 95\% CI $[-0.1150,+0.6478]$ points that includes zero. Hybrid $-$ random is $-4.0406$ points, 95\% CI $[-4.8926,-3.2626]$, wholly below zero. Returning-A (A2) CERs are $50.8346\%$ (full), $50.7402\%$ (hybrid), and $56.8819\%$ (random). The A2--A1 forgetting difference hybrid $-$ full is $-0.7086$ points, 95\% CI $[-3.3541,+1.0373]$ (includes zero); hybrid $-$ random is $-4.5257$ points, 95\% CI $[-8.3818,-1.5038]$ (wholly below zero). The pre-registered hybrid gate required at least a $0.3$-point CER improvement over full with a CI wholly below zero, and a significant advantage over random. Only the randomized-control criterion holds: the point estimate versus full moves in the wrong direction and the CI includes zero. Therefore the hybrid route is a development NO-GO: target-holdout2 is not run, and no additional hybrid seed or sweep is performed. What can be claimed is that the target-conditioned location signal is real and significant relative to the matched random control. What cannot be claimed is that using that signal as a full-replay auxiliary improves CER or forgetting on this stream. The elevated rollback count under random support is reported as an observation only and is not given a mechanism attribution. No confirmatory holdout number is available.

With the static arm completed on the same target-dev2 stream (development-only; 804 samples, all updates skipped, no feedback used; protocol checks include attempt~1, 33 history entries, final checkpoint processed-samples count of 804, matching UID/order/hashes, zero errors, and three checkpoints), static CER is $61.2747\%$ ($13{,}633/22{,}249$). \subsection{Budget-Matched Active Queries Do Not Beat Simple Selection on Target-Dev3}

Target-dev3 completes the same-stream update-source controls and the active-query gate on a disjoint development stream (758 clips; speakers 120/176/183). All six arms passed the pre-specified integrity checks at attempt~1: 758 ordered UIDs, 31 history entries, three retained checkpoints, final checkpoint processed-samples count of 758, matching manifest/sidecar/base/configuration hashes, and zero logged errors. These are development-only results, not test or holdout evidence.

Figure~\ref{fig:dev3_active_feedback} summarizes the paired analysis. Static CER is $72.7151\%$. Pseudo-only, feedback-only, combined-periodic, combined-random, and combined-uncertainty reach $69.7410\%$, $69.5489\%$, $69.2272\%$, $68.9348\%$, and $68.9557\%$, respectively. Every adaptive arm improves over static with its paired interval wholly below zero. In particular, pseudo-only minus static is $-2.9741$ CER points (95\% CI $[-3.5956,-2.3803]$), feedback-only minus static is $-3.1662$ points ($[-3.8669,-2.5262]$), and combined-periodic minus static is $-3.4879$ points ($[-4.4859,-2.7286]$). This establishes that either update source can help on this stream. It does not establish a significant interaction: combined-periodic minus pseudo-only is $-0.5138$ points ($[-1.2986,+0.1809]$), and combined-periodic minus feedback-only is $-0.3216$ points ($[-0.8525,+0.1296]$).

The active-query candidate fails four effectiveness conditions despite an exactly matched budget of 75 queries for periodic, random, and uncertainty. Uncertainty minus periodic is $-0.2715$ CER points, 95\% CI $[-0.6846,+0.1474]$: the point estimate misses the required $-0.3$ points and the interval crosses zero. Uncertainty minus random is $+0.0209$ points, 95\% CI $[-0.4158,+0.4543]$, so uncertainty is slightly worse on the point estimate and is not significantly better. Its queried true-error rate is $97.333\%$, versus $98.667\%$ for random; the paired-window difference is $-1.333$ points, 95\% CI $[-4.000,0.000]$, opposite to the required positive interval. The binary target is near saturation because 750 of 758 static predictions contain at least one character error, so this result rejects the gate on this stream rather than establishing a general defect of uncertainty estimation. Finally, uncertainty minus periodic static-corrected forgetting is $-0.1139$ points, 95\% CI $[-1.0394,+0.7927]$; its upper bound exceeds zero and therefore does not establish non-degradation under the pre-registered rule.

The adapter adds 75,265 parameters to a 284,275,490-parameter base model, or $0.0265\%$ of the base, and these are the only updatable parameters in adaptive arms. Across the five adaptive arms, measured process throughput spans 0.498--0.563 samples/s, peak allocated GPU memory spans 1.785--1.787~GiB, and the three retained checkpoints occupy 2.716~MiB. The final target-dev3 decision is therefore \textsc{no-go}: no holdout2 run, active-query seed expansion, or policy sweep is performed, and holdout2 remains frozen and unread.

\subsection{Current Decision}

The evidence supports continuing with a single-adapter application paper using full-sequence replay. The error-localized candidate fails its replay gate despite significantly outperforming randomized support. The current dynamic expert implementation is also a no-go under both diagnostic regimes: the original router collapses, while the validation-calibrated router fragments and is significantly worse than one adapter. The pre-specified gates stop localized test evaluation, further expert seeds, and parameter sweeps. Next-round development evidence on target-dev2 provides an exploratory, non-significant partial comparison of feedback-only versus combined replay. Full-sequence replay significantly improves both overall CER and static-corrected revisit forgetting relative to static on this stream. The hybrid route remains a NO-GO: the CTC-error hybrid significantly beats random support but does not improve CER or static-corrected forgetting relative to full-sequence replay under the pre-registered thresholds. Target-dev3 completes the update-source split and shows that pseudo-only and feedback-only learning each improve over static, but their combined increment is not significant. Its budget-matched uncertainty policy also fails all four effectiveness criteria against periodic and random selection. Consequently both new candidate routes stop at development, holdout2 remains frozen and unread, and no seed expansion or parameter sweep is allowed. Until a future fixed candidate passes its own development gate, these additions remain development evidence and do not alter the selected replay conclusion in this section.
