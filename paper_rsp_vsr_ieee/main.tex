\begin{abstract}
Visual speech recognition (VSR) models are usually evaluated as static predictors, although deployed users, cameras, and speaking conditions vary over time. We study continual personalization of a frozen Mandarin VSR backbone when one character-level correction is available every ten utterances. We use a strictly prequential protocol: each transcript is scored before its correction can update a 75k-parameter residual adapter. Pseudo-label updates are admitted only after CTC-based reliability checks; candidate updates then undergo post-update checks and can be atomically rolled back. We also evaluate a dynamic expert bank intended to isolate recurring domains. On a 3,908-utterance Chinese-LiPS test stream, one adapter reduces character error rate (CER) from 69.30\% to $66.88\pm0.10$\% over three seeds. The expert bank reaches $66.83\pm0.08$\%, only 0.055 percentage points better. On an A--B--C--A test revisit stream, 741 of 742 samples route to expert~0. A validation-only routing repair passes its signature-separability proxy gate, but fragments into eight experts, worsens CER by 2.306 points relative to one adapter, and reuses the first-domain expert for only 42.3\% of returning samples. Corrupting 10\% of feedback characters changes CER by only 0.041 points at the selected operating point, but rollback and reliability ablations do not support a strong causal robustness claim. These results support sparse-feedback adapter personalization, but not the present dynamic-expert mechanism. We report both conclusions to separate reproducible application value from unsupported structural novelty.
\end{abstract}
